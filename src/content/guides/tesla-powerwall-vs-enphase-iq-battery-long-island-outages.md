If you lost power for four days after a nor'easter and want to know which battery unit actually covers your house through it, the answer depends on two things: how much of your load you want to keep running, and whether your solar system can recharge the battery during the outage. Here is how the Powerwall 3 and Enphase IQ Battery 5P compare on the factors that matter in PSEG-LI territory.

## Capacity and Output: What Each Unit Can Actually Power

**Tesla Powerwall 3** ships as a single integrated unit with 13.5 kWh usable capacity, 11.5 kW of continuous AC output, and a 22 kW peak for motor-start loads. That peak matters on Long Island: a 3-ton central AC compressor draws 5–7 kW at startup. The Powerwall 3 handles it without tripping the backup circuit. The unit also includes a built-in solar inverter rated at up to 97.5% efficiency in DC-coupled configurations, which eliminates the need for a separate solar inverter if you're pairing with a new array.

**Enphase IQ Battery 5P** is 5 kWh per module, stackable up to four units. A single module puts out 3.84 kW continuous; a four-unit stack reaches 15.36 kW. The modular design means you size to your actual load rather than committing to a single 13.5 kWh block. A 10 kWh two-unit stack often makes more economic sense for a house that only needs to cover a critical load panel rather than the whole home. The IQ 5P uses the same IQ8X microinverter architecture as Enphase's solar products, so the system is fully Enphase-native when paired with IQ8 microinverters.

> The Powerwall 3's 22 kW peak output is the real differentiator for whole-home backup on a larger Long Island house. Enphase catches up in capacity flexibility — stack four 5P units and you have 20 kWh, but the continuous output per unit stays at 3.84 kW.

## Outage Runtime: Long Island Load Scenarios

For a 1,800 sq ft house in Huntington running essentials — refrigerator, lights, fans, router, one window AC unit drawing 1,200W — a full Powerwall 3 lasts approximately 8–10 hours without solar recharge. With a paired 6 kW solar array recharging during the day, the same setup covers indefinitely through a multi-day outage, provided the sun comes out for a few hours each day.

A two-unit Enphase IQ 5P stack (10 kWh) covers the same essential load for 6–8 hours on battery alone. Add a 6 kW array in Enphase microinverter configuration, and recharge speed is similar to the Powerwall setup because both systems have roughly equivalent charge rates in AC-coupled operation.

The difference becomes clear with whole-home coverage. If you want the central AC running through a summer storm outage, the Powerwall 3's 11.5 kW continuous output can handle it alongside other loads. A single IQ 5P unit at 3.84 kW cannot run central AC while simultaneously powering kitchen appliances. You would need a three- or four-unit stack to approach comparable whole-home coverage, which changes the cost comparison substantially.

For the typical Long Island scenario — a 2,000 sq ft Colonial built between 1970 and 1995, with gas heat, central AC, and a 200A service panel — the Powerwall 3 is the simpler path to true whole-home backup. Enphase's answer requires planning which loads you want to protect and sizing accordingly.

## Coupling Architecture: What It Means for Your Install

Enphase IQ 5P is **AC-coupled**. It connects to your main panel on the AC side, which means it works with any existing solar inverter — string inverters, microinverters, even a third-party system. The battery itself contains a built-in IQ8X microinverter. For Long Island homes with an existing solar array that isn't Tesla-based, AC coupling is the lower-friction retrofit path because you do not replace the existing inverter.

Powerwall 3 is **DC-coupled** in new solar installations. It integrates directly with a solar array at the DC level through Tesla's Gateway 3 unit, which acts as the system controller, utility meter interface, and islanding switch. DC coupling is slightly more efficient — one fewer AC-DC conversion step — but requires a Tesla-certified installer and the Gateway hardware. Retrofitting a Powerwall 3 onto an existing non-Tesla solar system is possible using AC coupling mode, but budget an additional $1,500–$2,500 for the Gateway 3 and commissioning by a certified installer.

## NEC 706, PSEG-LI Interconnection, and Local Permits

Battery storage installations in New York must comply with **NEC 706** (energy storage systems), which governs cell spacing, ventilation, and disconnecting means. Long Island installations in PSEG-LI territory also require the utility's interconnection application for grid-connected storage — typically a 30–60 day approval process for systems under 25 kW. The [install process page](/process/) covers what the PSEG-LI application involves and typical timeline from contract to energization.

For permits: **Town of Huntington Building Department** requires a building permit for any battery storage system, with inspection of the electrical connections and panel labeling per NEC 706.15. **Town of Hempstead** and **Nassau County** have similar requirements. Permit fees typically run $150–$400 depending on system size. Both Powerwall and Enphase installations require a licensed master electrician as the installer of record on the permit application in most Long Island jurisdictions.

Both units are UL 9540-listed, which is the safety standard most Long Island building departments require before issuing a permit.

## Why Long Island Outages Test Home Battery Systems Harder Than Most Markets

PSEG-LI serves approximately 1.1 million customers in Nassau and Suffolk counties through a grid that has some of the highest outage-per-customer metrics in New York State. Superstorm Sandy in 2012 left roughly 90% of PSEG-LI customers without power for periods ranging from 24 hours to 18 days. Since 2012, PSEG-LI has invested in grid hardening — underground cables, automated switching, and storm-hardened substations — but Long Island's geography creates persistent vulnerabilities: salt-air corrosion on overhead infrastructure, coastal flooding that takes out substations, and dense tree canopy on residential streets that account for a disproportionate share of outage incidents.

The practical implication for a battery sizing decision: Long Island is not a market where you design for a 4-hour outage. Multi-day outages during winter nor'easters and late-summer hurricanes are a realistic scenario. A battery that covers essentials for 8 hours on a cloudy day is a different product than one that bridges 72+ hours through solar recharge cycles.

Both the Powerwall 3 and Enphase IQ 5P include automatic grid-forming capability — when grid power drops, the battery takes over within milliseconds without a manual transfer switch. NEC 706.20 governs disconnecting means for energy storage systems in grid-outage backup configurations, and both units are listed to UL 9540A for fire safety in the islanding mode required for PSEG-LI interconnection approval.

## Warranty and Monitoring Comparison

**Powerwall 3:** 10-year warranty, unlimited cycles, with a minimum 70% capacity retention guarantee at end of warranty. Tesla's app provides real-time monitoring of solar production, battery state of charge, and grid import/export on a 15-minute interval. If the unit's capacity falls below 70% within the warranty period, Tesla replaces it. The app also includes a "Storm Watch" mode that pre-charges the battery to 100% when a severe weather event is forecast in your zip code — this integrates with PSEG-LI's outage prediction feed for certain storm events.

**Enphase IQ 5P:** 10-year warranty, unlimited cycles, with similar 70% end-of-warranty capacity retention guarantee. Monitoring runs through the Enphase Enlighten platform — per-microinverter production data for the solar array, battery state of charge, and a detailed log of grid vs battery vs solar consumption split. For homeowners who already use Enlighten for an existing Enphase solar system, consolidating battery monitoring in the same dashboard avoids running two separate apps and two service contracts. Enphase does not offer a native storm pre-charge feature comparable to Powerwall's Storm Watch, though the battery can be manually charged to 100% through the app before an anticipated outage.

## Incentives in PSEG-LI Territory

Both units qualify for the **30% federal Investment Tax Credit** (IRA Section 48) on the full installed cost when charged primarily from solar. New York's **25% state solar tax credit** (up to $5,000) applies to both when paired with a new solar array. PSEG-LI's **VDER** program credits both battery types for grid exports and demand response participation. The [full incentives breakdown](/incentives/) has current NYSERDA and VDER figures — they update quarterly as program funding changes.

Effective installed cost after federal and state credits on a combined solar-plus-storage project:
- **Powerwall 3**: $12,500–$15,000 pre-incentive → approximately $6,500–$8,500 net
- **IQ 5P (single unit)**: $8,500–$11,000 pre-incentive → approximately $4,500–$6,000 net
- **IQ 5P (two-unit stack)**: $15,000–$19,000 pre-incentive → approximately $8,000–$10,500 net

These ranges include PSEG-LI interconnection fees and local permit costs. They do not include the solar array itself.

## Which One Fits Your Situation

**Choose Powerwall 3 if:**
- You want whole-home backup with a single unit, including central AC
- You are installing a new solar array and want DC-coupled efficiency
- You are comfortable with Tesla's ecosystem and service network

**Choose Enphase IQ 5P if:**
- You have an existing non-Tesla solar system and want an AC-coupled addition
- You prefer to start with one unit and add capacity in future years
- You run an existing Enphase microinverter array and want consolidated monitoring

Both units have Long Island installation track records and trained local contractors who carry them. The choice usually comes down to existing system compatibility and how much whole-home coverage you need in a multi-day outage scenario.
